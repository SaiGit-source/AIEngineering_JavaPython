# Financial Comparison of TD Bank and Royal Bank of Canada

## Overview  
This report compares the financial performance and valuation metrics of TD Bank (TD.TO) and Royal Bank of Canada (RY.TO).

## Key Financial Metrics  
| Metric                | TD Bank (TD.TO)     | Royal Bank (RY.TO)   |
|-----------------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| Current Price         | $148.20              | $251.06              |
| Trailing P/E Ratio    | 12.00                | 17.24                |
| Dividend Yield        | 2.96%                | 2.66%                |
| Price to Book (P/B)  | 2.02                 | 2.73                 |
| Total Revenue         | $65.98 billion       | $63.42 billion       |
| Return on Equity (ROE)| 17.82%               | 15.40%               |

## Analysis  
- **Price-to-Book Ratio**:  
  TD Bank has a lower P/B ratio (2.02) compared to Royal Bank (2.73), indicating that TD Bank may be undervalued relative to its book value.

- **Trailing P/E Ratio**:  
  TD Bank’s trailing P/E ratio is 12.00, which is lower than Royal Bank’s ratio of 17.24. This suggests that investors are paying less for each unit of earnings for TD Bank than for Royal Bank.

- **Dividend Yield**:  
  TD Bank has a higher dividend yield (2.96%) compared to Royal Bank (2.66%). This may be appealing to investors looking for income.

- **Return on Equity (ROE)**:  
  TD Bank has a ROE of 17.82%, compared to Royal Bank’s 15.40%, suggesting that TD Bank is more efficient in generating profits from its equity.

## Conclusion  
In summary, TD Bank shows stronger performance in terms of lower valuation ratios and higher returns on equity compared to Royal Bank of Canada. Investors may find TD Bank a more attractive investment option based on these metrics.
